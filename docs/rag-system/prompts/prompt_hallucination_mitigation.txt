### Section 4 — Layer 3: Post-Generation Faithfulness Scorer

#### 4.1 Purpose
Explain the role of Layer 3 as the detection layer — it does not prevent hallucinations but quantifies the risk that a generated response contains unsupported claims. It gives the user a verifiable signal and highlights the specific sentences to check.

Critically: Layer 3 does NOT block the response. Explain the design decision — in a clinical research context, suppressing a response may be worse than flagging it for review.

#### 4.2 Algorithm
- File: src/faithfulness.py
- Class: FaithfulnessChecker
- Method: FaithfulnessChecker.check()
- Embedding model: reuses HuggingFaceEmbeddings instance already in session state — no additional model download required

Document the exact algorithm as a numbered sequence:

  Step 1: Split the generated response into individual sentences
  Step 2: For each sentence s_i:
            embed sentence → vector v_i
            for each retrieved chunk c_j:
              compute cosine_sim(v_i, embed(c_j))
            score_i = max cosine similarity across all chunks
  Step 3: overall_score = mean(score_i for all sentences)
  Step 4: Apply thresholds and surface results in UI

Present the core formula as a formula block:

  score_i = max { cosine_sim(embed(s_i), embed(c_j)) }
              j ∈ retrieved_chunks

  overall_score = (1/n) × Σ score_i
                            i=1..n

### Section 7 — Known Gaps & Future Work

Be explicit about what is NOT implemented. This section is especially important for clinical governance reviewers. For each gap, state clearly what the risk is and what would close it:

⚠️ NLI-based entailment checking
- Risk: cosine similarity scoring can miss directional errors (a sentence affirming something the context denies may still score highly due to vocabulary overlap)
- What would close it: a dedicated NLI model (e.g., cross-encoder) to verify claim direction against retrieved chunks

⚠️ Automatic response blocking
- Risk: low-faithfulness responses are displayed with a warning but are not suppressed — a user may proceed without reading the warning
- What would close it: a configurable faithfulness gate that replaces the response with a refusal message when overall_score falls below a hard threshold

⚠️ Self-consistency sampling
- Risk: a single generation pass may produce a confident but unsupported answer that scores reasonably on faithfulness
- What would close it: generating N responses and flagging high variance as a hallucination risk signal
